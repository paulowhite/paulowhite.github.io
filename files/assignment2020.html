Task

Prepare a "poster" presentation which you will present on the last course day (November 30, 2020). In exceptional cases you might present on another day. To get an exception send an email to the course director: pabl at sund dot ku dot dk.

Do not make a real poster but instead read carefully the following instructions.

Mandatory instructions

IMPORTANT: You should use data of your own PhD project or data related to your PhD project obtained from your colleagues or supervisor. If you cannot obtain such data, you will obtain data from the course organizer, but please let him know ASAP.

  1. Prepare at most 6 slides which you can present in about 6 minutes.
  2. Hand in a pdf or powerpoint presentation (can also be a html presentation) by email to pabl at sund dot ku dot dk until November 18, 2020.
  3. The presentation has to include elements of each of the following sections
    • Research question – including a definition of the background population.
    • Material/Data
    • Statistical methods – can be short but should cover all the statistical methods that are used produce the Results.
    • Results:
      • At least one descriptive statistic but can be more, e.g., a table with descriptive statistics for several variables (by groups).
      • A graph which either shows the raw data or the results of the statistical analysis, as appropriate.
      • At least one conclusion sentence based on statistical inference of the data which includes a confidence interval and p-value.
  4. Figures and tables are only acceptable if they come with a proper caption (legend).
  5. The file has to be named assigment2020-your-full-name.ppt or .pdf or .html.
  6. If you cannot attend the last course day send an email to pabl at sund dot ku dot dk ASAP.

Further instructions

  1. The statistical part does not have to be complex. Keep it simple, so that you are sure that you understand the methods that you apply. Choose a research question which requires a complex analysis only if you feel that you can explain everything.
  2. You do not have to use all variables in the data set and you are allowed to subset the data to simplify the problem. To simplify the analysis you may also categorize a continuous variable.
  3. The result section must include at least one p-value and one confidence interval. However, note that to achieve this it is sufficient to perform a single comparison of an outcome variable between two groups.
  4. All decimal numbers should be in English notation and suitably rounded.
  5. The methods section must include the units of the variables and the name of the statistical method which is used to calculate the p-value.
  6. For all results that are shown in your poster presentation you should be able to explain what they mean.
