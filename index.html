Biography

I am an assistant professor in biostatistics at the University of Copenhagen, Denmark. I work at the Department of Cardiology at Gentofte univeristy hospital and at the Section of Biostatistics of the university. My main activities are are methodological research, providing statistical support to medical researchers and teaching. Most of my current collaborators are working on cardiology research projects based on registry (big) data. Their projects often involved competing risks analysis, causal inference and risk prediction. My initial research topics mainly focused on survival and competing risks data and the development and validation of prediction models. More recent research topics include causal inference, multiple testing, design of group sequential clinical trials and empirical likelihood inference. Overall, my research ranges from applied biostatistics, epidemiology and clinical research, to projects in theoretical and computational developments in biostatistics.

SAfJR

We organized the 8th annual conference Survival Analysis for Junior Researchers (SAfJR) in Copenhagen, Denmark, on 24–26 April 2019. It was a three-day event that was aimed at career-young statisticians with an interest in the application and development of time-to-event analysis and related topics. See the webpage.
