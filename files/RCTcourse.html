Prerequisites

The course is tailored for Ph.D.-students in health sciences who already have taken the Ph.D.-course "Basic Statistics for Health Researchers" or "Statistics for experimental medical researchers" or students who have a similar knowledge about both statistical thinking and R coding. Students with a more limited background are expected to benefit from the course too, but to a lesser extent.

How to prepare for the course

Internet

You will work with your own laptop and you will need Internet during the course. It is your own responsibility to be able to connect, but you should be able to use the wireless net of the university, eduroam (login needed).

Statistical software

The main focus of this course is not on how to use statistical software. But statistical software is needed for all data analyses and examples that illustrate the statistical methods. The free statistical software R will be used throughout the course, e.g., via R-studio (https://posit.co/downloads/).

The participants are expected to use their own laptop during the course, to have installed all relevant software and to have downloaded all data for use during the course.

A minimum level of familiarity with basic R is considered as a prerequisite to best benefit from the course. See above.
